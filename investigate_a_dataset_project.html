Introduction¶

  • This dataset collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment.
    • A number of characteristics about the patient are included in each row.
      ### The data describtion
  • 'ScheduledDay' column: it tells us on what day the patient set up their appointment.
  • 'Neighborhood' column: it indicates the location of the hospital.
  • 'Scholarship' column: it indicates whether or not the patient is enrolled in Brasilian welfare program Bolsa Família.
  • 'No-show' column: it says ‘No’ if the patient showed up to their appointment, and ‘Yes’ if they did not show up.

# Creating a ploting function to plot a bar chart for 4 variables 
def ploting(title_, y1, y2, y3, y4, var1, var2, var3, var4):
    q4_series = pd.Series({y1: var1,
                          y2: var2,
                          y3: var3,
                          y4: var4})
    plt.barh(q4_series.index, q4_series.values)
    plt.xlabel('Ratio(%)', fontsize = 14)
    plt.title(title_, fontsize = 16)
    plt.gca().invert_yaxis()
    plt.show();

Conclusions¶

According to this data analysis, The following points were found:

  • One of the most common diseases in Brazil -according to this data- is Hypertension.
  • It is clear that the best practice to increase patients' attendance, is to make the waiting period within 10 days.
  • The longer waiting period is, The less attendance we get.
  • In the oher hand, the highest number of cancellation occurs wihin 10 days too and decreases by time which needs more investigation to find out the root cause for this.
  • There is no relationship between gender and attendace or cancellation as men and women have the same ratio of attendance and cancellation.
  • There is no clear relationship between attendance and cancellation for patients with and without scholarship.
  • Patients who have scholarship attended less and cancelled more, Parients who don't have scholarship attended more and cancelled less.
  • The very old and old patients' attenace rate is very high and their cancellation is the lowest amonge the rest of age categories.
  • The most common hospital location in the data is JARDIM CAMBURI.
  • There is no clear evidence to say that scholarship and gender have an effect on the attendence.

Limitation¶

During The analysis, the following limitations were found:

Issues with the dataset:¶

  • This is only a sample of the data wich implies uncertainty, a different sample may lead to different results; this was clear when the analysis showed that one of the most common diseases in Brazil -according to this data- is Hypertension.
  • The highest number of cancellation occurs wihin 10 days and decreases by time, the same happens with the number of attendance which needs more investigation to find out the root cause for this.
  • Some missing and incorrect values were dropped from the data which may affect the analysis's results.

Issues with the method of analysis:¶

  • Some missing and incorrect values were dropped from the data instead of replacing them with the correct values.
  • No statistical inference was performed in the analysis to test the signicance of the results.
  • More analysis could be performed in the data but this is not possible due to data limitation.
